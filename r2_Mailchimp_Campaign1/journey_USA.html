How to take your head shots
The dermatolagist will need to assess your 6 head shots. In order to track your hair regrowth we also recommend you take additional sets of 6 shots every 3 months. Make sure the lighting conditions are exactly the same every time. This will give you the best idea of your progress (i.e., avoid shadows, use natural lighting if available, be mindful of glare).

Your head shots should be taken from a distance that fits the entire head and scalp within the frame. It may be helpful to have someone assist you.


1. Front of the head.
Face forward and look directly at the camera.
2. Back of the head.
Face behind with head held straight.


3. Right side of the head.
Your hairline and temple regions should be clearly visible. If your hair is long enough to cover your ears, comb or draw it back.
4. Left side of the head.
Your hairline and temple regions should be clearly visible. If your hair is long enough to cover your ears, comb or draw it back.

In addition you will need 2 shots at an angle:

5. Front of the head.
Looking down at an angle of approximately 45 degrees, please take 1 photo of the front of your hairline, ensuring that your temples are visible. If your hair is long, pinning or drawing it back may be necessary to avoid obscuring the hairline.
6. Back of the head.
Looking up at an angle of approximately 45 degrees, please takea photo of the back of your head.
